📊 Inventory Dashboard Analysis

Project Overview

This project showcases a dynamic **Inventory Management Dashboard** built in Microsoft Excel to analyze stock performance, demand trends, and supply chain efficiency. The dashboard enables interactive exploration of inventory data using slicers and timeline filters, allowing users to monitor key metrics and make data-driven decisions. The solution integrates **Pivot Tables, Pivot Charts, and VBA automation** to create a professional, interactive interface. Key performance indicators such as **Total Units Sold, Inventory Turnover, Stockout Rate, and Average Stock Levels** provide a quick overview of operational efficiency. Through this analysis, the dashboard highlights important business insights, including high-performing product categories, warehouse-level demand differences, supplier lead time variability, and stockout risks in specific categories. These insights help identify opportunities to improve inventory planning, optimize stock distribution, and enhance supplier reliability. This project demonstrates strong skills in **Excel-based data analysis, dashboard design, and business problem-solving**, making it a practical example of how raw data can be transformed into meaningful insights for real-world decision-making.

Tools Used

Microsoft Excel

Key Insights

  • Total sales reached 136,024 units, indicating steady demand with seasonal variation
  • THigh inventory turnover reflects efficient stock utilization
  • Stockout rate is low (1.1%), but still impacts key product categories
  • Food and Stationery are top-performing categories, while Furniture shows low movement
  • Nagoya Depot leads in sales, highlighting regional demand differences
  • Supplier lead times vary, indicating inconsistent supplier performance
  • Higher stockouts in Electronics and Clothing suggest need for improved forecasting

Business Recommendation

  • Improve demand forecasting for high-variability categories
  • Optimize stock allocation across warehouses
  • Reduce dependency on high lead-time suppliers
  • Maintain higher safety stock for fast-moving products
